# Academic Writing Samples

## Master's Thesis

My MA Thesis was completed in fulfillment of the requirements for a Master of Arts degree in English literature from the University of British Columbia. It was supported by a Social Sciences and Humanities Research Council Grant awareded by the Canadian Federal Government in January 2022.

My thesis focuses on the formation and standardization of Blues music across America during the Great Migration, specifically after President Abraham Lincoln created the Emancipation Proclamation in 1863, to right before the beginning of the Harlem Renaissance in 1920. This project begins with an examination of historical background at the end of the Transatlantic slave trade into the Great Migration, along with highlighting the importance of Black folktales and oral storytelling within Blues music, and ethnography practices of collecting and preserving Black cultural materials. The second half of the project is an analysis of poetry and fiction texts either written during the 1863–1920-time period or that are fictionally set in the plantation South, for evidence of Blues-related song forms or characters that could represent Blues musicians.

[The Blues: Intersections between Black Art Forms and Post-Emancipation American Literature](http://hdl.handle.net/2429/87896)
